When and Why

Whenever I go on planes, I like to give myself coding challenges to try and complete on the plane before the plane lands or my laptop dies, whichever happens first. In late October of 2017, I got the chance to fly from California to the East Coast, and for that flight, I decided to try programming a virtual version of Leonardo Da Vinci.

At the time, though, it seemed really difficult to program an artificial intelligence with creativity, so I went with the next best thing: a genetic algorithm.

General Genetic Algorithms


In essense, Genetic Algorithms need a few basic components to work. A genetic algorithm attempts to get better at doing some task or get closer to some goal by sort of mimicking the process of evolution.

Let's begin with the idea of actual evolution. It's been commonly accepted that evolution takes place over generations, where two organisms, like say two fish reproduce. Their baby fish will have half the DNA of each parent, and also some random mutations to allow for small adaptations, so that over millions of years, adaptations that are beneficial and improve the fish's fitness in its environment will get spread across the population.

The genetic algorithm breaks down this theory and uses the basic components to generate approximate solutions for achieving of a goal. Basically, you need to have:

  1. "Organisms" that breed and carry the "genetics"
  2. "DNA" that get's passed from parent to child
  3. "Population" which is basically a ton of organisms at once.
  4. "Breeding" that takes half the DNA from each "parent" with some random mutations
  5. "Fitness" which is a measure of how well the organism does at the task
The task then becomes, to make a painter, what do I map to each of these five basic features.

Details of My Genetic Painter Algorithm


For my case, the organisms are objects that hold the DNA, which I will call "painters", are in charge of breeding, and get evaluated on their fitness for how well suited they are for the task at hand. This task, I remind you, is to draw a a given picture with random shapes and colors.

Next, the DNA that each painter has will just be a huge list of shapes. Each shape keeps track of its vertices and its color. The DNA is basically the recipe for how to build the picture that the painter can paint. For our purposes, each painter can only paint on painting.

The population is basically just a big list of painters to choose from.

The breeding will go through the DNA for each parent and randomly select one shape from each parent in order with equal probability. Then each shape will have some small chance of "mutating" or basically just changing slightly.

Finally, we have a fitness function. This basically draws the picture and calculates the RMSE where the error is euclidean distance of each drawn picture to the pixel of the true picture. The fitness is approximately the inverse of this RMSE.
